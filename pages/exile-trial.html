Love in Exile

In Dapitan, Rizal found unexpected love with Josephine Bracken, who came seeking treatment for her father's eyes. Their relationship became a bright light in his exile, though they were never officially married due to his refusal to retract his writings.

The Final Days

The Trial

December 26, 1896: Faced charges of rebellion, sedition, and conspiracy

The Verdict

December 29, 1896: Condemned to death despite the lack of direct evidence

The Dawn

December 30, 1896: Faced his destiny at Bagumbayan Field with dignity

Final Moments

In his final hours, Rizal showed remarkable composure. He wrote his last poem, "Mi Último Adiós," a powerful farewell to his beloved country. He requested to face the firing squad, but was denied. His last words were "consumatum est" (it is finished), spoken as he fell with his face to the morning sky.
